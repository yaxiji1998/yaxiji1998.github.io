resultsSection

Posted on 2020-12-07 In 英语
Symbols count in article: 4.3k Reading time ≈ 4 mins.

definition

In the results section of the research paper, you can report the research results based on the information collected by the applied methodology. The results section should simply state the findings, without prejudice or explanation, and arranged in a logical order. The result part should always be written in the past tense. If your paper includes data obtained from your own research, the section describing the results (also called “discoveries”) is especially necessary.

Structure and method

For most research paper formats, there are two ways to display and organize results.

  1. Present the results, and then provide a brief explanation of the results. For example, you may notice an abnormal relationship between two variables during analysis. It is correct to point out this in the results section. However, speculating on why this association exists and providing hypotheses about what might happen is the discussion part of your paper.
  2. Propose one part, then discuss it, then propose the next part, then discuss it, and so on. This is more common in longer papers because it helps readers better understand each discovery. In this model, it can be helpful to provide a short conclusion in the results section that links each discovery together and links to the discussion.

contents

  • The body of the result is composed of text

    1. Use text to guide readers to key results
    2. The text should not repeat the content of the chart, only need to describe the key results and trends

  • Present the experimental results in an orderly manner according to the questions or assumptions in the preface

  • Contains important negative conclusions

    If you do not get the expected results, it may mean that your hypothesis is incorrect and needs to be reformulated, or you have accidentally discovered an unexpected situation that requires further research. Moreover, in many cases, no effect may be obvious. In any case, even if others do not support your hypothesis, your results may be important to others. Don’t fall into the trap of thinking that the opposite of what you expect is necessarily “bad data.” If you are doing well, they are just your results and need to be explained. Many important findings can be traced back to “bad data.”

    1. Your assumption needs to be revised
    2. Encountered unexpected things that require further research
    3. Your results may be important to others
    4. Give an explanation for the negative conclusion

  • What is the key result

    1. Can answer the questions raised in the preface through analysis
    2. The data after analysis may not be the key result, but it can let us know what the key result is
    3. Key results may include
      1. Clear trend
      2. similarity
      3. Major difference
      4. Relevance
      5. Maximum and minimum values,

  • Analyze and integrate data into charts

    1. Organize the results section according to the order of the chart
    2. Each chart needs to include a brief description of the results
    3. The necessary information is placed in the legend
      1. The legend of the table is above the table
      2. The legend of the image is placed below the image

principle

  1. Describe results concisely and objectively
  2. Important negative conclusions should also be included
  3. For each result, the method part must have a corresponding method
  4. The summary analysis of the data can be presented in the form of text, graphs or tables
  5. The text should be used to complete the chart. The text should not repeat the content of the chart, but only need to describe the key results and trends
  6. Do not present the same data in the chart
  7. Must tell the reader the information expressed in each graph
  8. Arrange the order of the charts carefully
  9. When referring to a chart in the text, Figure can be abbreviated as Fig., and Table can never be abbreviated
  10. The legend of the table is above the table, because the table is usually read from top to bottom, and the legend of the image is placed below the image, because the image is usually read from bottom to top
  11. Passive voice dominates this part, use active voice as much as possible
  12. Don’t use raw data when it can be represented by mean, percentage, etc.

artifice

  1. Present in an orderly manner, use the outline as a reference, and describe in the order of the method section
  2. When writing a draft, mark one or two key results from the chart below the chart
  3. The text of the result part is written according to the order of the chart

Writing steps

We have summarized twelve steps to complete the writing of the academic paper results part

  1. Decide which results to present based on the questions asked in the introduction (do not include all the results you get or observe)

  2. Determine the format of the organization data

    • According to the order presented in the method section
    • Importance of results

  3. Determine the best representation of your data

    • text
    • Figure
    • form

  4. Summarize your findings and indicate relevant data

  5. Describe the results that do not appear in the chart

  6. Clearly describe the response or difference

  7. Ensure data is accurate and consistent

  8. Summary statistical analysis

  9. When referring to your results, use the past tense

  10. Number the charts in the order they appear in the text

  11. Provide a title for each chart (each chart must be complete)

  12. Precise, concise and clear description
